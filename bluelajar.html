PROBLEM

Many secondary school students find that relying solely on traditional learning at school falls short of meeting their learning needs. This condition not only hinders their progress but also creates a barrier in reaching their full potential. Furthermore, seeking additional academic support outside the classroom can be difficult and expensive.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

For the final assignment of the course Business Management (CSIM601190), my teammates (Andresha Pradana, Amanda Nadhifah Zahra Andini, Shafa Aleyda Tsabitah, Syarief Ahmad Almuhajir) and I created B;uelajar, an online platform that connects secondary school students to tutors and provides interactive learning materials. In this project, I assumed the role of Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and was tasked with creating the prototype of our digital solution. With B;uelajar, students can easily find tutors who specialize in various subjects and receive personalized learning materials tailored to their academic needs.

HIGH-FIDELITY DESIGN

With the insights gained through our user research, I proceeded to the design phase of B;uelajar's high-fidelity interface. One of the key considerations in this phase was tailoring an interface design that resonates with our target market (secondary school students). The goal was not only to achieve aesthetic appeal but also an intuitive and user-friendly design.

HIGH-FIDELITY DESIGN (DESKTOP)

TAKEAWAYS

This project was my first opportunity to utilize my interface design skills in a group assignment setting. It presented a set of challenges, mainly stemming from the limited depth of our user research, which made it somewhat challenging for us to elicit comprehensive requirements. Given more time, I undoubtedly would have delved deeper into the user research process to gain a more profound understanding of the needs and preferences of our target audience. This increased understanding would have allowed me to craft a more tailored and user-centric user interface, ultimately optimizing the overall user experience. Overall, the personal connection I had to the task made this project a memorable one; I was designing an app that addresses a problem I personally encountered during my secondary school years.
