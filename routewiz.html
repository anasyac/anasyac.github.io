PROBLEM

In today's era of abundant travel recommendations, individuals often struggle to select a destination that caters to their unique preferences and interests due to a lack of personalization. Moreover, many people heavily rely on maps and weather apps for their commute, but juggling between those apps can be impractical for some.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

For the final assignment of the course Human-Computer Interaction (CSGE602024), my teammates (Katrina Gisella Sembiring, Muhammad Raditya Hanif, Viena Hollanda Koswara, Yeira Putri Nandika) and I created RouteWiz, a platform that seamlessly integrates mobility, weather, and mapping features into a single, user-friendly solution. With the help of AI, RouteWiz goes beyond traditional functionalities by offering personalized travel recommendations tailored to each user's unique preferences and interests.

In this project, I took charge of crafting the home page and recommendation page, overseeing the design system, and leading the overall design process. This involved navigating through concepting, execution, and evaluating the platform to meet predefined requirements.

DESIGN PROCESS

The design process of RouteWiz followed the principles of User-Centered Design (UCD) and The Four Basic Activities of Interaction Design, which according to Sharp, Preece, & Rogers (2015), involves four key stages: Establishing Requirements, Designing, Prototyping, and Evaluating. These stages are carried out iteratively with the main focus of putting the intended users of RouteWiz at the center of its design and development.

USER INTERVIEW

To better understand our target users, we conducted 1:1 semi-structured user interviews with 6 different participants aged 19-51 as our primary research method. The interviews are conducted online through video conferencing platforms such as Zoom and Google Meet. Through these interviews, we were able to gain our potential users' perspectives on mobility, weather, and mapping apps as well as their pain points and behaviors when commuting.

NOTABLE INTERVIEW QUOTES

"I feel disappointed when the information I receive about a tourist destination is not accurate."
"When I'm driving, using multiple applications simultaneously is challenging because it's distracting."
"Since I seek varied experiences, my preferences tend to change frequently, making travel recommendations less accurate for me."

EVALUATING

As we progressed to the evaluation phase, we utilized both Usability Testing (UT) and the System Usability Scale (SUS) as our evaluation methods. UT provided valuable insights, sparking several improvement ideas to incorporate in the next iteration of the product. In addition, our SUS evaluation involving 40 respondents resulted in a score of 75, which falls into the 'Good' category. This implies that while RouteWiz exhibits good usability, there are identified areas for improvement. These findings lay the groundwork for refining RouteWiz in future iterations.

TAKEAWAYS

RouteWiz stands out as my most intricate design project to date. While certain details may not be documented here, I think that this description of RouteWiz captures the core of our approach. This project underscores the importance of user-centered design and the integral role of user feedback in creating effective digital solutions. Involving users not only improves the overall quality of the design but also ensures that the final product resonates with their needs and preferences. Leading the design process was a new opportunity for me, and I am honored to be acknowledged for it. In hindsight, I see that more time dedicated to exploring the interface design would've allowed us to think more critically about how the modules integrate within the app, thereby producing a more refined work. Nevertheless, I take pride in the collaborative efforts poured on this project and the valuable lessons learned throughout the journey.
