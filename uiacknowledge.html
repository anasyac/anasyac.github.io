PROBLEM

Universitas Indonesia Achievement Community (UIAC), a student unit dedicated to achievement development and post-campus coaching for UI students, faces challenges in effectively managing knowledge within their organization due to the absence of a Knowledge Management System (KMS).

PROJECT OVERVIEW

For the final assignment of the course Knowledge Management (CSIE604180), my teammates (Fitria Dwi Cahya, I.M. S. Salahuddine El Ayyubie, Katrina Gisella Sembiring, Yeira Putri Nandika) and I created UIACKnowledge, a comprehensive Knowledge Management System designed specifically for UIAC. With UIACKnowledge, UIAC members can now centralize and organize their resources, making it easier to discover, capture, share, and apply knowledge across the organization.

USER INTERVIEW

We conducted a user interview with Feizya Hilyata Musaddiq, Vice President of UIAC for the year 2023. The aim of the interview was to gather firsthand insights as to how UIAC operates on a daily basis and how the organization manages its knowledge. During the discussion, we focused on understanding the challenges UIAC faced in terms of knowledge management prior to the implementation of UIACKnowledge. Additionally, we explored improvement ideas for their existing knowledge management mechanisms and how these could be seamlessly implemented in UIACKnowledge, taking into account the workflows and operational nuances of UIAC. The insights that we were able to gather from this conversation were instrumental in making sure that our solution not only addressed existing challenges but also facilitated a more effective and efficient knowledge management process within the organization.

KEY FINDINGS

01

Like most student organizations, the management period of UIAC lasts only for a year. Therefore, it is crucial for UIAC 2023 to adopt proper knowledge management mechanisms so that knowledge within the organization can be passed on from the outgoing members to the incoming ones.

02

UIAC 2023 possesses mostly tacit knowledge, one example being the time management skills of its members. These skills are utilized to assist UIAC 2023 in selecting and prioritizing various work programs.

03

Currently, UIAC 2023 implements knowledge management solely through Google Drive, with folders designated for each division. However, this approach results in unstructured and non-acomodative knowledge management.

HIGH FIDELITY DESIGN

As we moved on to the design process, each team member was given a specific responsibility in creating the user interface of UIACKnowledge. I took charge of designing the To-Do page, as well as the Monthly and Weekly Calendar pages. We incorporated a selection of colors to correspond with UIAC 2023's established palette, ensuring a consistent visual design that aligns with the organization's branding.

TAKEAWAYS

For me, this project brought a uniquely real experience, almost as if we were designing for an actual client. Teaming up with UIAC to tackle their knowledge management challenges brought a sense of realness and significance to our work. The journey was both rewarding and challenging, as it demanded that we sync up our expectations with those of UIAC. I found the process of designing a product with a focus on knowledge management personally fascinating. This experience deepened my appreciation for the multifaceted role of product designers and the many hats they wear. It made me realize that being a product designer goes way beyond aesthetics; it's about understanding the whole picture – from user needs to operational workflows to the goals of the client – in order to create solutions that hit the mark.
